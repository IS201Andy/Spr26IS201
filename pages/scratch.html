Rock Climbing

    I've probably gone rock-climbing as many times here at college as I had previously in my life! There's ample opportunity, the community is welcoming, the difficulty is approachable yet significantly progressive, and it's great exercise.

  • Helpful Gear
    • Helmet
    • Strong rope
    • Belaying device
    • Climbing-specific carabiners
    • (At least one) climbing partner
    • Powdered chalk
    • Climbing shoes (very tight, very smooth, and very sticky)

  • Climbing Styles; Indoor vs Outdoor
    • Indoor gyms feature custom-built walls with interchangeable plastic holds of various shapes and sizes. Routes are generally overlapping and color-coded by difficulty.
    • Bouldering (low-level climbing without ropes), clip climbing (carrying additional carabiners, anchors, and screws to support your own rope as you climb), and top-rope (climbing toward your rope's anchor point, with a belay partner to assist in rappelling down) are common across disciplines. Crack climbing is more prevalent outdoors, where there may be no significant holds at all!

  • Difficulty Ratings
    • Number.number.letter (Y.D.S.)
      The Yosemite Decimal System is the most common climbing grade system in North America and uses two numbers separated by a period - often with a letter attached - such as 5.10d or 5.11a. This subdivides climb attributes and indicates terrain, gear and experience requirements, and potential consequences of a fall.
    1. The first number is always a 5 for traditional rock climbing. This exposure rating means protective equipment is required (lower numbers are for hiking and higher numbers are impossible unassisted). Slopes are often near or past vertical and any fall from a 5 route could be fatal.
    2. The second number indicates difficulty and required experience. Up to 5.7 is beginner-friendly, 5.8-5.10 (including letters) are intermediate routes for the 'weekend warriors', and anything past 5.11 is advanced.
    3. The letters serve to augment the second number for difficulty evaluation and sometimes blur the lines... for example a 5.10c or d can feel much closer to a 5.11a or b than to its own 5.10a or b
